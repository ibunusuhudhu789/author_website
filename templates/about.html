The Beginning


I didn't become a writer because I had all the answers. I became one because words were the only place where silence finally made sense.

Stories were never just stories to me. They became a way to survive emotions I couldn't explain out loud — loneliness, longing, fear, obsession, love, grief, hope. What started as random thoughts slowly turned into worlds, characters, and confessions hidden between lines.

I write the kind of stories that linger. The kind that ache a little. The kind that feel dangerously honest. Under the name Ibunu Suhudhu, I create stories filled with flawed people, emotional chaos, forbidden love, vulnerability, darkness, tenderness, and the strange beauty of being human.

"Some stories are written to entertain. Mine are written to linger — somewhere between obsession, loneliness, and the things people are too afraid to admit out loud."

The Writing Life


Writing, for me, is not a hobby. It's a second existence. Every character I create carries a piece of something real — a fear I understand, a wound I remember, or a desire I was too afraid to speak about.

I love writing emotionally intense stories where relationships feel messy, intimate, and painfully alive. From A Fate Unwritten to the stories still waiting to be written, every project begins with a single feeling I can't ignore.

I don't want readers to simply read my stories. I want them to feel trapped inside them.

Life Outside the Page


Writing may be where my emotions live, but development is where my mind feels most alive. Beyond fiction, I spend my time building things — websites, systems, ideas, and experiences that begin as nothing more than a thought on a screen.

As a developer, I enjoy turning concepts into something real and interactive. Whether I'm working on design, backend logic, or experimenting with new technologies, I love the process of creating something meaningful from scratch.

In many ways, writing and development are not that different to me. One builds worlds with emotions, while the other builds them with logic. I simply happen to love both.
